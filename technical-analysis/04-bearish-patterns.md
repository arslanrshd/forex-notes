# Bearish Candlestick Patterns

Bearish candlestick patterns suggest that **sellers are gaining control** and the price may move downward. They are most reliable when they form near **resistance** levels.

> **Note:** A bearish pattern does not guarantee a reversal. Always wait for confirmation from the next candle or use additional technical analysis.

---

## Hanging Man

**Structure:**

* Small body near the top
* Long lower wick (at least twice the body size)
* Little or no upper wick

**Meaning:**

Although buyers pushed the price higher, strong selling pressure appeared before the candle closed. It may signal a potential bearish reversal if confirmed by the next candle.

---

## Shooting Star

**Structure:**

* Small body near the bottom
* Long upper wick
* Little or no lower wick

**Meaning:**

Buyers pushed the price higher, but sellers rejected the move and forced the price back down, indicating potential bearish momentum.

---

## Bearish Engulfing

**Structure:**

* First candle: Small bullish candle
* Second candle: Large bearish candle that completely engulfs the previous candle's body

**Meaning:**

Strong selling pressure has completely overcome the previous buying pressure.

---

## Bearish Harami

**Structure:**

* First candle: Large bullish candle
* Second candle: Small bearish candle completely inside the previous candle's body

**Meaning:**

Buying pressure is weakening, and sellers may be starting to gain control.

---

## Evening Star

**Structure:**

1. Large bullish candle
2. Small indecision candle
3. Large bearish candle closing deep into the first candle's body

**Meaning:**

A classic three-candle bearish reversal pattern showing a shift from buyers to sellers.

---

## Three Black Crows

**Structure:**

* Three consecutive long bearish candles
* Each candle opens within the previous candle's body
* Each candle closes lower than the previous candle

**Meaning:**

Strong and sustained selling momentum, indicating sellers are in control.

---

## Key Points

* Most reliable when forming near **resistance** levels.
* Wait for the pattern candle to **close** before making a trading decision.
* Confirmation from the next candle is strongly recommended.
* Combine with **Support & Resistance**, **Trend Analysis**, or other technical tools for better confirmation.

## Summary

Bearish candlestick patterns indicate increasing selling pressure and may signal the beginning of a downward price movement when confirmed by additional market context.
