# Continuation Candlestick Patterns

Continuation candlestick patterns suggest that the **current trend is likely to continue** after a brief pause or period of consolidation.

> **Note:** A continuation pattern does not guarantee that the trend will continue. Always wait for the pattern to close and confirm it with the overall market trend.

---

## Marubozu (Bullish / Bearish)

**Structure:**

* Long, solid candle body
* Little or no upper wick
* Little or no lower wick

**Meaning:**

One side dominated the entire trading session. In a strong trend, a Marubozu indicates strong momentum in the direction of the trend.

---

## Rising Three Methods (Bullish)

**Structure:**

1. Large bullish candle
2. Two to four small bearish candles that remain within the range of the first candle
3. Large bullish candle closing above the first candle's high

**Meaning:**

A temporary pullback occurs, but buyers quickly regain control and continue the uptrend.

---

## Falling Three Methods (Bearish)

**Structure:**

1. Large bearish candle
2. Two to four small bullish candles that remain within the range of the first candle
3. Large bearish candle closing below the first candle's low

**Meaning:**

A temporary pullback occurs, but sellers regain control and continue the downtrend.

---

## Rising Window (Bullish Gap)

**Structure:**

* A gap forms between two bullish candles.
* The second candle opens above the previous candle's high.

**Meaning:**

Strong buying momentum. The gap often acts as a future support zone.

---

## Falling Window (Bearish Gap)

**Structure:**

* A gap forms between two bearish candles.
* The second candle opens below the previous candle's low.

**Meaning:**

Strong selling momentum. The gap often acts as a future resistance zone.

---

## Key Points

* Most reliable during established trends.
* Avoid relying on continuation patterns in sideways markets.
* Wait for the pattern to **close** before making a trading decision.
* In Forex, gap patterns (Windows) are uncommon during normal trading and are more likely to appear after the weekend market open.
* Combine with **Trend Analysis**, **Support & Resistance**, or other technical tools for better confirmation.

## Summary

Continuation candlestick patterns help traders identify temporary pauses in a trend and potential opportunities to trade in the direction of the prevailing market trend.
