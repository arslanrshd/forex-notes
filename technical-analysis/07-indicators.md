# Technical Indicators

Technical indicators are mathematical calculations based on **price**, **volume**, or both. They help traders analyze market conditions, identify trends, measure momentum, and identify potential entry or exit points.

> **Note:** Indicators are based on historical price data, so they generally lag behind the market. They should be used to confirm price action, not replace it.

---

## Moving Average (MA)

**Purpose:**

Smooths price data by calculating an average price over a selected period to identify the overall trend.

### Types

**Simple Moving Average (SMA)**

* Calculates the average price equally across the selected timeframe.

**Exponential Moving Average (EMA)**

* Gives more weight to recent prices, making it react faster to market changes.

### Usage

* **Price above MA:** Bullish trend
* **Price below MA:** Bearish trend
* Often acts as **dynamic support** or **dynamic resistance**.

---

## Relative Strength Index (RSI)

**Purpose:**

A momentum oscillator that measures the speed and strength of price movements.

### Range

* **0 – 100**

### Key Levels

* **Above 70 (Overbought):** Price may be overextended and could experience a pullback or reversal.
* **Below 30 (Oversold):** Price may be overextended to the downside and could experience a bounce or reversal.

### Usage

Helps traders identify potential trend exhaustion and overextended market conditions.

---

## Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

**Purpose:**

A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages.

### Components

* **MACD Line**
* **Signal Line**
* **Histogram**

### Usage

* **Bullish Crossover:** MACD line crosses above the Signal line.
* **Bearish Crossover:** MACD line crosses below the Signal line.

---

## Bollinger Bands

**Purpose:**

Measures market volatility and identifies relatively high or low price levels.

### Components

* **Middle Band:** 20-period Simple Moving Average (SMA)
* **Upper Band**
* **Lower Band**

### Usage

* **Bands widen:** High volatility
* **Bands narrow (Squeeze):** Low volatility
* Price touching the upper or lower band may indicate an overextended move, but it is **not** a reversal signal by itself.

---

## Key Points

* Avoid using multiple indicators that provide the same information.
* Overbought and oversold levels work best in ranging markets and may remain extreme during strong trends.
* Wait for candlestick or market structure confirmation before acting on an indicator signal.
* Combine indicators with **Support & Resistance**, **Chart Patterns**, and **Price Action** for better confirmation.

## Summary

Technical indicators help traders analyze trends, momentum, and volatility while filtering market noise. They are most effective when used as confirmation tools alongside price action and sound risk management.
