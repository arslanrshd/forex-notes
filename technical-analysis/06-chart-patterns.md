# Chart Patterns

Chart patterns are formations created by price movements on a chart. They help traders identify potential **trend continuations** or **trend reversals**.

> **Note:** Chart patterns do not guarantee future price movement. Always wait for a confirmed breakout candle close and use additional technical analysis.

---

## Double Top

**Structure:**

* Two consecutive peaks at a similar price level
* A temporary pullback between the peaks
* Price breaks below the pullback low (neckline)

**Meaning:**

A classic bearish reversal pattern indicating buyers failed to push the price higher, allowing sellers to gain control.

---

## Double Bottom

**Structure:**

* Two consecutive lows at a similar price level
* A temporary rally between the lows
* Price breaks above the rally high (neckline)

**Meaning:**

A classic bullish reversal pattern indicating sellers failed to push the price lower, allowing buyers to gain control.

---

## Head and Shoulders

**Structure:**

* Left shoulder (first peak)
* Higher head (second and highest peak)
* Right shoulder (third peak, lower than the head)
* A neckline connecting the two swing lows

**Meaning:**

A powerful bearish reversal pattern showing that an uptrend may be losing momentum.

---

## Inverse Head and Shoulders

**Structure:**

* Left shoulder (first low)
* Lower head (second and deepest low)
* Right shoulder (third low, higher than the head)
* A neckline connecting the two swing highs

**Meaning:**

A powerful bullish reversal pattern showing that a downtrend may be losing momentum.

---

## Ascending Triangle

**Structure:**

* Flat horizontal resistance
* Rising support line

**Meaning:**

A bullish continuation pattern where buying pressure gradually increases until resistance is broken.

---

## Descending Triangle

**Structure:**

* Flat horizontal support
* Falling resistance line

**Meaning:**

A bearish continuation pattern where selling pressure gradually increases until support is broken.

---

## Symmetrical Triangle

**Structure:**

* Lower highs
* Higher lows
* Two converging trendlines

**Meaning:**

A neutral pattern showing consolidation between buyers and sellers. It can break out in either direction but often continues in the direction of the existing trend.

---

## Flag (Bullish / Bearish)

**Structure:**

* Sharp price move (flagpole)
* Small consolidation channel (flag)
* Breakout in the direction of the previous trend

**Meaning:**

A continuation pattern indicating a temporary pause before the trend resumes.

---

## Pennant (Bullish / Bearish)

**Structure:**

* Sharp price move (flagpole)
* Small symmetrical triangle (pennant)
* Breakout in the direction of the previous trend

**Meaning:**

A continuation pattern showing a brief consolidation before the trend continues.

---

## Wedges

### Rising Wedge

**Structure:**

* Rising support and resistance lines
* Converging trendlines

**Meaning:**

Often a bearish pattern. It may signal a reversal in an uptrend or a continuation in a downtrend.

### Falling Wedge

**Structure:**

* Falling support and resistance lines
* Converging trendlines

**Meaning:**

Often a bullish pattern. It may signal a reversal in a downtrend or a continuation in an uptrend.

---

## Key Points

* A chart pattern is confirmed only after a breakout candle closes outside the pattern.
* Avoid entering trades before the pattern is fully formed.
* In Forex, candle close confirmation is generally more reliable than relying on volume alone because volume data is decentralized.
* Combine chart patterns with **Support & Resistance**, **Candlestick Patterns**, and **Trend Analysis** for better confirmation.

## Summary

Chart patterns help traders recognize recurring market formations that may signal trend continuations or reversals. They are most effective when confirmed by breakout price action and other technical analysis tools.
