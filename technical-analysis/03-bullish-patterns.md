# Bullish Candlestick Patterns

Bullish candlestick patterns suggest that **buyers are gaining control** and the price may move upward. They are most reliable when they form near **support** levels.

> **Note:** A bullish pattern does not guarantee a reversal. Always wait for confirmation from the next candle or use additional technical analysis.

---

## Hammer

**Structure:**

* Small body near the top
* Long lower wick (at least twice the body size)
* Little or no upper wick

**Meaning:**

Sellers pushed the price lower, but buyers rejected the decline, regained control, and closed the price near the top.

---

## Inverted Hammer

**Structure:**

* Small body near the bottom
* Long upper wick
* Little or no lower wick

**Meaning:**

Buyers attempted to push the price higher. It may signal a potential bullish reversal if confirmed by the next candle.

---

## Bullish Engulfing

**Structure:**

* First candle: Small bearish candle
* Second candle: Large bullish candle that completely engulfs the previous candle's body

**Meaning:**

Strong buying pressure has completely overcome the previous selling pressure.

---

## Bullish Harami

**Structure:**

* First candle: Large bearish candle
* Second candle: Small bullish candle completely inside the previous candle's body

**Meaning:**

Selling pressure is weakening, and buyers may be starting to gain control.

---

## Morning Star

**Structure:**

1. Large bearish candle
2. Small indecision candle
3. Large bullish candle closing deep into the first candle's body

**Meaning:**

A classic three-candle bullish reversal pattern showing a shift from sellers to buyers.

---

## Three White Soldiers

**Structure:**

* Three consecutive long bullish candles
* Each candle opens within the previous candle's body
* Each candle closes higher than the previous candle

**Meaning:**

Strong and sustained buying momentum, indicating buyers are in control.

---

## Key Points

* Most reliable when forming near **support** levels.
* Wait for the pattern candle to **close** before making a trading decision.
* Confirmation from the next candle is strongly recommended.
* Combine with **Support & Resistance**, **Trend Analysis**, or other technical tools for better confirmation.

## Summary

Bullish candlestick patterns indicate increasing buying pressure and may signal the beginning of an upward price movement when confirmed by additional market context.
