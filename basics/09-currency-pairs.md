# Currency Pairs

## Definition

A **currency pair** is the quotation of one currency against another.

It shows how much of the **quote currency** is needed to buy **one unit of the base currency**.

## Structure

```text
Base Currency / Quote Currency
```

**Example:**

```text
EUR/USD
```

* **EUR** → Base Currency
* **USD** → Quote Currency

## Types of Currency Pairs

### Major Pairs

Major pairs always include the **US Dollar (USD)**.

**Examples:**

* EUR/USD
* GBP/USD
* USD/JPY

### Minor Pairs

Minor pairs do **not** include the US Dollar.

**Examples:**

* EUR/GBP
* GBP/JPY
* AUD/NZD

### Exotic Pairs

Exotic pairs consist of one major currency and one currency from an emerging economy.

**Examples:**

* USD/TRY
* USD/MXN

## Key Points

* Forex is always traded in **currency pairs**.
* Every pair has a **base currency** and a **quote currency**.
* Currency pairs are classified as **Major**, **Minor**, or **Exotic**.

## Summary

A currency pair compares the value of one currency with another and is the basic unit of trading in the Forex market.
